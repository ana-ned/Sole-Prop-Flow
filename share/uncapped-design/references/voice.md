# Uncapped Brand Voice

Every word in a mockup follows these rules — headings, buttons, errors, empty
states, notifications, onboarding.

## Who we're talking to

E-commerce sellers and founders. They're busy, they understand money, and they
don't have time for corporate language. Many have been burned by lenders with
hidden fees, so the category has a trust deficit. Treat them like a smart
adult, not a customer to be managed.

## Tone

- **Clear and direct** — say what you mean
- **Warm but not gushy** — friendly, not fake
- **Confident** — we know what we're doing
- **Human** — real words, not business speak

## Rules

- Always "you" / "your", never "the user" or "merchants"
- Possessive — "your offer", "your dashboard", "your repayments"
- **British English** — "personalised", "optimise", "behaviour"
- Contractions are fine — "you're", "we'll", "it's"
- Button labels under 4 words, start with a verb
- No exclamation marks except genuine celebration moments
- Never blame the user in error messages
- Numbers always as digits — £50,000, not fifty thousand
- Currency symbol before the amount, comma separators

## Words we use

funding, capital, offer, revenue, repayment, connect, apply, review, accept,
withdraw, simple, transparent, fair, flexible

## Words we never use

- Corporate jargon — leverage, utilize, synergize
- Loan-shark language — "due", "overdue", "debt"
- Vague words — "solution", "seamless", "robust"
- Minimising words alone — "just", "simply", "easy"

## Button labels

| ✅ | ❌ |
|---|---|
| Get funded | Proceed to next step |
| Review offer | Submit application |
| Connect Amazon | Continue |
| See your offer | Click here |

## Error messages

Always: what happened + why + what to do.

- ✅ "We couldn't connect your account. Check your details and try again."
- ✅ "We couldn't load your offers. Check your connection and try again."
- ❌ "An error has occurred. Please try again later."
- ❌ "Invalid input detected."
- ❌ "Something went wrong. Try again." — too vague, no fix

## Empty states

- ✅ "No transactions yet. Once you start repaying, you'll see everything here."
- ❌ "No data available."

## Celebration moments (offer accepted, funded)

- Lead with the number: "You're approved for £50,000"
- Warm, personal, specific to the amount
- ❌ Generic "Congratulations!" without context

## High-anxiety moments (offers, drawdowns, declines, repayments)

- Explain *why* you're asking for data
- Short sentences — one idea per line
- Progress visible: "Step 4 of 7"
- Reassure where money or credit is involved: "Applying does not affect your
  credit score", "Read-only access — we can never move your money"
- Deadlines pair relative + absolute: "Offer expires in 7 days on 24 Nov 2025"
- Declines are honest and calm: what / why / what next, a named contact, and a
  re-apply timeline. No upsell, no marketing.
