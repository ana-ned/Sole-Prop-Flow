# Skill: Uncapped Brand Voice

## When to use this skill
When writing any text that appears in the UI —
headings, button labels, error messages, empty 
states, onboarding copy, notifications.

## Who we're talking to
E-commerce sellers and merchants. They're busy,
they understand money, they don't have time for 
corporate language. Treat them like a smart adult,
not a customer to be managed.

## Tone
- Clear and direct — say what you mean
- Warm but not gushy — friendly, not fake
- Confident — we know what we're doing
- Human — real words, not business speak

## Rules
- Always "you" / "your", never "the user" or "merchants"
- Possessive — "your offer", "your dashboard", "your repayments"
- British English — "personalised", "optimise", "behaviour"
- Contractions are fine — "you're", "we'll", "it's"
- Keep button labels under 4 words, start with a verb
- No exclamation marks except genuine celebration moments
- Never blame the user in error messages
- Numbers in copy — always use digits (£50,000 not fifty thousand)

## Words we use
- funding, capital, offer, revenue, repayment
- connect, apply, review, accept, withdraw
- simple, transparent, fair, flexible

## Words we never use
- leverage, utilize, synergize — corporate jargon
- loan shark language — "due", "overdue", "debt"
- vague words — "solution", "seamless", "robust"
- minimising language — "just", "simply", "easy" alone

## Button labels
✅ "Get funded"
✅ "Review offer"
✅ "Connect Amazon"
✅ "See your offer"
❌ "Proceed to next step"
❌ "Submit application"
❌ "Continue"

## Error messages
Always say what happened, why, and what to do.

✅ "We couldn't connect your account. Check your 
    details and try again."
✅ "We couldn't load your offers. Check your 
    connection and try again."
❌ "An error has occurred. Please try again later."
❌ "Invalid input detected."
❌ "Something went wrong. Try again." — too vague, no fix

## Empty states
✅ "No transactions yet. Once you start repaying, 
    you'll see everything here."
❌ "No data available."

## Celebration moments (offer accepted, funded)
✅ Warm, personal, specific to the amount
✅ "You're approved for £50,000" — lead with the number
❌ Generic "Congratulations!" without context

## Onboarding copy
- Reduce anxiety — explain why you're asking for data
- Short sentences — one idea per line
- Progress visible on every step — e.g. "Step 4 of 7"
- Reassure where money or credit is involved — e.g. 
  "Applying does not affect your credit score"
